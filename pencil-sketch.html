Pencil Sketching

Overview: Pencil sketching is the most basic and versatile form of sketching. It involves using graphite or colored pencils to create drawings ranging from simple outlines to highly detailed artwork.
Tools:
  • Graphite pencils (HB, 2B, 4B, etc.)
  • Colored pencils
  • Erasers (kneaded and standard)
  • Blending stumps or tissues
Techniques:
  • Hatching and Cross-Hatching: Use lines to create texture and depth.
  • Shading: Gradually build tones for a realistic effect.
  • Blending: Smooth transitions between light and dark areas.
Applications:
  • Portraits
  • Still life
  • Architectural sketches
Tips:
  • Practice light hand movements to control shading.
  • Start with light lines and gradually build darker tones.
