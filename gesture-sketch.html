Gesture Sketching

Overview: Gesture sketching involves quick, loose drawings to capture the movement, energy, and essence of a subject. Itâ€™s often used as a warm-up exercise or for practice.
Tools:
  • Graphite pencils or pens
  • Newsprint or sketching paper
Techniques:
  • Continuous Line Drawing: Keep your hand moving without lifting the pencil.
  • Quick Sketches: Focus on speed rather than detail.
  • Energy Lines: Use flowing lines to show motion.
Applications:
  • Figure drawing
  • Animal sketches
  • Storyboarding
Tips:
  • Set time limits for each sketch to train your speed.
  • Focus on capturing the overall form rather than details.
